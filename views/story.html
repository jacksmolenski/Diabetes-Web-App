Story

My story begins back in 2003 when I was diagnosed with Type 1 Diabetes at the ripe age of 15 months old. My parents found out because I was peeing and drinking a lot. They took great care of me. Would trade nights with each other to make sure I was doing okay. I am super grateful for that. One of my earliest diabetes memories is funny looking back on it now, and that is when I was getting my pumpsite inserted on my buttcheek. I just remember squirming because I was not happy about it.

Fast forward a bit of time into elementary school. I received a para to assist me with my diabetes. Her name was Mrs. LaCombe. I only have great memories of her. She was super kind and so helpful with my diabetes at that age. At this stage of my diabetes, every kid was was asking what it was and that never bothered me much. I quickly learned that I could use it to my advantage. I would get to eat snacks or leave things I didn't want to do. As a kid I never associated diabetes with anything bad.

I used to go on Junior Diabetes Research Foundation (JDRF) walks with my family. Those were always a blast. Family, friends, and even some of my father's coworkers would come out to support me and the cause. Another thing I am super grateful to have had with such wonderful support. Some of them even took place at Six Flags. I was somewhat scared of roller coasters, but I still enjoyed that day each year very much.

The diabetes never stopped me from doing what I wanted. I played soccer, basketball, or really anything outside that I could've as a kid. I would know right away when my blood sugar was low and would have to pause to treat it, but it never stopped me.

All of this was in the time without a CGM, so I had so many little finger prick holes on my fingers. When the CGM first was available to me, that was pretty game changing. That made keeping my blood sugars stable much easier.

For the more interesting side of things, here is how my doctors and I have laughed but disagreed based off of how I take care of myself. When I correct my high blood sugars, I just enter carbs because it is fastest. They want me to enter the blood sugar but my correct ratios have been off for years since I never use them. But my A1C and blood sugars are always great so they don't want me to change what I am doing.

To jump ahead to now, I am a young adult, and I am excited for the future and hopeful to help those struggling with their diabetes!
